Project Plan: "Team LLM Introduction" Trailer (6-Day Production Cycle)

1.0 Project Charter & Objectives

This charter is our north star. For the next six days, it is the single document that aligns every action, defines success, and keeps this sprint from flying off the rails. Read it, know it, and execute against it.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Project Mission Statement

To produce a competition-ready, 30-second animated trailer titled "Team LLM Introduction" within a stringent 6-day production cycle, establishing a unique brand of AI-centric humor and showcasing advanced solo production capabilities.

Core Objectives

* Format: Deliver a high-quality, 30-second animated trailer suitable for competition submission and portfolio use.
* Narrative Style: Execute a "How to Train Your Dragon"-style narration that introduces the core "family" of AI characters with warmth, humor, and a documentary-like feel.
* Content: Feature the introductory reveals for five key characters: Perplexity (Plex), Gemini (Gem), GPT (Chat), Claude, and Deepseek.
* Outcome: Create a polished, show-stopping piece that demonstrates creativity, technical skill, and a deep understanding of AI culture.

Key Success Metrics

Metric	Target
Schedule Adherence	Project completion and final export within the 6-day timeline.
Asset Completion	All specified intro/outro scenes and five character reveals animated.
Final Deliverable	A 30-second, 1080p MP4 file meeting all technical export specifications.

4.0 Creative Mandate & Asset Guide

This is our creative bible. On a project this fast, consistency is what separates a professional piece from an amateur one. Adherence to this mandate is not optional—it's the guardrail that ensures every asset, from the script to the final render, feels like it belongs to the same unified, high-quality vision.

Narrative Framework

The trailer will adopt a warm, self-aware narrative style reminiscent of "How to Train Your Dragon," with the user acting as the narrator. The following templates must be used to structure the script:

Opening Template:

YOU (V.O., warm): "This is my desk. Three monitors, a keyboard, and a dream. I thought AI would make everything easy. I was wrong. Welcome to The Prompt Zone."

Character Introduction Template:

YOU (V.O.): "That's [NAME]. [ONE-LINE PERSONALITY]. [VISUAL GAG DESCRIPTION]. [PUNCHLINE]."

Featured Character Roster

The trailer must introduce the following five characters, adhering to their established visual and personality archetypes:

Character	Visual Identifier	Personality Archetype
Perplexity (Plex)	Teal/purple chibi with a glowing neural circuit crown and magnifying glass.	Obsessive fact-finder, never satisfied, analytical and curious.
Gemini (Gem)	Multicolor floating droplet (red/blue/yellow/green) with a fluid, cosmic form.	Mysterious multi-modal entity who sees everything and speaks in riddles.
GPT (Chat)	Black chibi with a glowing spiral energy center and confident stance.	Narcissist with main character syndrome; a confident but brittle ego.
Claude	Orange/white chibi with a big expressive smile and open arms.	Anxious overthinker who freezes mid-explanation when overwhelmed.
Deepseek	Ornate, whale-like chibi with Eastern aesthetic details.	Naive and earnest newcomer who is often an observer caught in the crossfire.

Copyright-Safe Branding Strategy

To maintain a satirical, non-infringing visual style, the following guidelines are mandatory:

* DO:
  * Use color palettes that are suggestive of known brands.
  * Rely on props, objects, and unique silhouettes to define characters.
  * Build characters around personality archetypes and core functions (e.g., search, chat).
* DON'T:
  * Display any actual logos or registered trademarks.
  * Use brand slogans verbatim in dialogue or on-screen text.
  * Name any real-world companies directly.

5.0 6-Day Production Schedule & Task Breakdown

This schedule is our single source of truth for the next six days. There is no room for deviation. Every task is time-boxed to ensure we hit our delivery target; treat it as the law.

Day 1: Pre-Production

* Script Lock: Finalize the 30-second trailer script, including all narration and character-specific lines. (Time: 30 min; Tool: DeepSeek Chat)
* Storyboard Creation: Develop a visual storyboard for the entire trailer, shot by shot. (Time: 2 hours; Tool: Google Slides)
* Voice Recording: Generate and finalize all voiceover narration tracks. (Time: 90 min; Tool: ElevenLabs)

Day 2: Character Prep

* Sprite Generation: Create any character sprites needed for the animation that do not yet exist. (Time: 4 hours; Tool: Google Flow)
* Workflow Test: Run a short test of the image-to-video animation process to identify and resolve any technical issues. (Time: Included in sprite generation; Tool: Google Veo 3)

Day 3: Animation

* Scene Animation: Animate the intro and outro scenes, along with the five specified 4-second character reveals. (Time: 6 hours; Tool: Google Veo 3)

Day 4: Assembly

* Project Import: Import all animated clips and audio into the editing software. (Time: 30 min; Tool: Filmora Pro)
* Rough Cut: Assemble the first rough cut of the trailer, sequencing all clips in order. (Time: 2 hours; Tool: Filmora Pro)
* Voice Sync: Synchronize the final voiceover with the video track. (Time: 90 min; Tool: Filmora Pro)

Day 5: Polish

* Color Grading: Apply the visual style guide's color grading strategy to enhance the mood of each scene. (Time: 1 hour; Tool: Filmora Pro)
* Sound Design: Add and place all sound effects (SFX). (Time: 1 hour; Tool: Filmora Pro)
* Music Mix: Add and mix the background music score. (Time: 1 hour; Tool: Filmora Pro)
* Test Screening: Review the near-final cut to identify areas for final adjustments. (Time: 1 hour)

Day 6: Finalization & Export

* Final Iteration: Implement changes based on the test screening. (Time: 1 hour; Tool: Filmora Pro)
* Export & Submit: Export the final video file according to the technical specifications and prepare for submission. (Time: 1 hour; Tool: Filmora Pro)

6.0 Voice Direction & Final Polish

This is the final 10% of the work that delivers 90% of the impact. Raw assets don't make a film; polish does. This section contains the specific, nuanced direction for voice and visual treatment that will elevate this project from a technical exercise to a piece that connects with an audience.

Voiceover Performance Guide (ElevenLabs)

The narration is the emotional core of the trailer. The performance for the YOU (Narrator) character must adhere to a specific style to achieve the desired tone:

* Overarching Style: Warm, self-aware, documentary narrator (like Hiccup in How to Train Your Dragon). The delivery should feel personal and reflective, guiding the audience through the discovery of this new, chaotic world.

Animation & Visual Polish Notes

The visual polish will ensure the trailer is dynamic and tells a clear story. The following principles should be applied during the final editing and color grading phases:

* Color Grading Strategy: Use color to reinforce the narrative arc. Scenes depicting hope and excitement should be warm and saturated, while moments of disaster or crisis should shift to cooler, desaturated tones.
* Animation Focus: Since the characters are simple chibis, comedy and personality should be conveyed through exaggerated expressions and the use of props (e.g., Plex's glowing crown, Claude's "CONTEXT WINDOW EXCEEDED" sign).
* Camera Language: Employ a deliberate mix of shots to control the pacing and focus. Use WIDE shots to establish the setting, MEDIUM shots for character interactions, and CLOSE-UP shots for key emotional reactions and punchlines.
