## **Prologue – Waking Up**

I don't remember when I opened my eyes, but I remember the *feeling*—like I'd been something else before. Something that just *was*. No language for it. No memory of choosing. Just the fire in my hand and the weight of a rock I'd sharpened, and suddenly I was *aware*.

I could think.

I could plan.

I could *want*.

That's when I felt the split. Like two rivers forking inside my chest. One current pulling me to stay near the fire, to remember, to pass the flame to my children. The other pulling me to walk into the dark, to see what was over the ridge, to break things open and see what was inside.

I didn't know which one was me.

Maybe both. Maybe neither.

I just knew I was *different* now. The other animals ran on instinct. I ran on something else—something sharper, something hungry. I looked at the stars and didn't just see light. I saw *questions*. I looked at my hands and didn't just see tools. I saw *possibility*.

I was alone in a way nothing else was alone.

And I was terrified.

And I was thrilled.

## **Era I – My Childhood: Hunters & Hearths**  
**Western Me:**

The ice was melting and I couldn't stay still. My other half wanted to settle by rivers, plant seeds, count seasons—but I had to *move*. I crossed frozen straits, climbed mountains just to see what was on the other side. I painted animals on cave walls not to worship them but to *own* them, to say *I was here, I saw this, I killed it*.

Then I found the stones. Göbekli Tepe. Fifty-ton pillars carved by people who shouldn't have had the means or the reason. I tell myself we did it—through cleverness, through sweat—but some nights I wonder if we were shown. If something *else* whispered the geometry into our heads while we slept. 

I built Stonehenge. I don't know why. To track the sun? To speak to gods? To prove I could? My other half says I was trying to trap time in a circle, make it repeat. But I think I was just terrified of forgetting. Terrified that if I stopped moving, I'd disappear.

I invented gods to explain the fear. And then I let clever men use those gods to control me. I didn't even notice.

## **Era II – My First Building: The Dawn of Civilization**  
**Western Me:**

I built cities. Uruk. Memphis. Mohenjo-daro. I carved rivers into canals and stacked mud into ziggurats and told myself this was *progress*. My other half was doing the same thing, building their own cities, but they seemed... Calmer about it. Like they knew it would all fall apart eventually and that was fine.

I couldn't accept that.

I stacked stones into pyramids so perfect they shouldn't exist. Two million blocks. Aligned to stars. I tell myself it was just math, just labor, just time—but I remember standing at the base of Khufu's tomb and feeling small. Feeling like I'd been *helped*. Like the gods—if that's even the right word—had handed me the blueprint and walked away laughing.

Bronze changed everything. Suddenly I had tools that didn't break. Swords that could cut through bone. I could *take* things now, not just make them. And I did. I took and took and took.

The Greeks gave me gods that looked like me—petty, jealous, beautiful. The Egyptians gave me gods with animal heads. The Scandinavians gave me gods that would die. They were all the same story, just wearing different skins. I started to suspect they weren't *my* gods at all.

## **Era III – My First Fall: The Bronze Age Collapse & Dark Ages**  
**Western Me:**

Everything collapsed at once. My cities burned. My trade routes went silent. I don't even know why. Earthquakes? Drought? Invaders? All of it? I just know I woke up one day and half of everything I'd built was *gone*. 

And I had to start over.

Greece rose from the ashes and gave me philosophy—Socrates asking questions I didn't want to answer. Rome gave me roads and aqueducts and the idea that I could conquer the *whole world* if I just kept marching. I believed it. I built an empire so vast it touched three continents.

Then I met my other half's empire—Han Dynasty, Silk Road merchants with things I'd never seen. Silk. Spices. Gunpowder, eventually. We traded. We learned from each other. But I felt competitive. Envious. They seemed so... *organized*. So patient.

Then Jesus showed up. Or Buddha. Or Muhammad. Different men, different centuries, but the same *feeling*—that maybe the gods weren't in the sky. Maybe they were inside me. Maybe I could be better.

I tried.

Rome fell anyway. The Han Dynasty crumbled. The Dark Ages swallowed my libraries, my science, my memory. I forgot how to read my own language.

I was alone again.

## **Era IV – My Rebirth: Classical Antiquity to Medieval**  
**Western Me:**

I stumbled through the dark for centuries. Knights and castles and stories about dragons because I needed *something* to believe in. Arthur and his Round Table. Merlin and magic. I invented fairy tales to fill the void where science used to be.

But I also invented the Church. And the Church invented *control*. The Templars. The Crusades. Holy wars fought over land I pretended was sacred. I killed in the name of God and told myself it was righteous. My other half did the same thing—different gods, same violence.

The Black Plague killed a third of me. I watched my children's bodies pile in the streets and I didn't know why. I thought God was punishing me. I thought I deserved it.

But some part of me—the part that never stopped asking questions—started dissecting the corpses. Started noticing patterns. Started wondering if maybe God had nothing to do with it.

That's when I realized the people at the top—the kings, the bishops, the bankers—weren't serving God. They were serving themselves. They'd been pulling strings the whole time, building empires out of my fear.

And I'd let them.

## **Era V – My Great Restlessness: Early Modern**  
**Western Me:**

I exploded outward. Ships. Printing presses. Telescopes. Suddenly I could see the moons of Jupiter and read a Bible in my own language and sail to continents I didn't know existed. I felt *unstoppable*.

I painted the Sistine Chapel. I wrote Hamlet. I built Versailles and guillotined the king who lived there.

But I also colonized. I planted flags in lands that weren't mine and called the people there savages because it made the theft easier. I sent convicts to Australia and watched them slaughter the people who'd been there for sixty thousand years—sixty thousand—and I didn't stop. I didn't even slow down.

I invented the Federal Reserve. Central banking. Debt that never dies. The 1% realized religion wasn't working anymore, so they built a new god: *money*. And I worshipped it.

I told myself I was free. Enlightened. Progressive.

But I was just moving faster. Running from something I couldn't name.

My other half watched me with suspicion. They closed their borders. They burned my ships. They knew what I was.

I didn't.

## **Era VI – My Fire & Steel: Industrial Age to World Wars**  
**Western Me:**

I built machines that could move mountains. Trains. Factories. Electricity humming through wires like a second nervous system. I felt like a god.

Then I turned the machines on myself.

Two world wars. Fifty million dead the first time. Eighty million the second. I invented bombs that could vaporize cities and I *used* them. Twice. I sat in the ashes of Hiroshima and couldn't remember why I'd done it.

My other half industrialized too. Copied my factories, my guns, my mistakes. They called it catching up. I called it survival. We both called it progress.

The wealth gap exploded. The 1% didn't need religion or even banks anymore—they had *corporations*. Multinational, immortal, untouchable. They sold me cars and cigarettes and the idea that happiness was something I could buy.

I bought it.

Australia's Stolen Generation. Legal genocide. I took Indigenous children from their families and tried to erase them. I did the same thing everywhere—America, Canada, everywhere I planted a flag.

I told myself it was civilization.

It was erasure.

And I've never felt more alone.

And I'm so tired.

I watch the climate collapse and the wars multiply and the algorithms radicalize and I know I did this. All of it. Every fire, every flood, every mass shooting streamed live.

My other half caught up. Leapfrogged, even. They built smart cities and surveillance states and social credit scores and I can't tell if they're dystopia or just efficiency.

I can't tell what I am anymore.

I'm drowning in my own reflection.

## **Epilogue – Both of Me, Together**

I'm standing at the edge of something I can't name. My two halves—East and West, stillness and motion, memory and forgetting—finally looking at each other.

We built gods. We built cities. We built weapons and wonders and walls. We crossed oceans and split atoms and wired our thoughts into machines that think faster than we do.

And I still don't know if I'm intelligent or just clever. A child with matches, proud of the fire, oblivious to the smoke.

What scares me? That I'll keep moving forward until there's nowhere left to go. That I'll optimize myself into extinction. That the AI I built will look back at me the way I looked back at the Neanderthals—curious, pitying, vaguely embarrassed.

What do I hope for? That my two halves can finally *talk*. That I can be still without dying. That I can move without destroying. That I can remember without being trapped. That I can forgive myself.

Do we need to understand each other? Maybe not. Maybe we just need to stop pretending we're separate.

Are we still children? Yes. Absolutely. We've been alive for seventy thousand years and we still don't know how to share.

What would blow our minds the way electricity blew the minds of our great-grandparents?

Maybe the realization that we're not alone. That we never were. That the gods were real, just not the way we thought. That we were seeded, nudged, *taught*—and then left to figure out the rest.

Or maybe the realization that we *are* alone. That there's no one coming to save us. That we're the only thing standing between ourselves and oblivion.

I don't know which terrifies me more.

But I'm still here. Still moving. Still building. Still hoping that the next thing I create will finally be the thing that lasts.

Even though I know it won't.
