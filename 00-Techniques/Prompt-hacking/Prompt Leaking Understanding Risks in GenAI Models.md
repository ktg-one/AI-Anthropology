Well, so what? Why should anyone care about prompt leaking?

Sometimes people want to keep their prompts secret. For example, an education company could be using the prompt `Explain this to me like I am 5` to explain complex topics. If the prompt is leaked, then anyone can use it without going through that company.

## Conclusion

Prompt leaking is an important concept to understand because the risk of unintentionally exposing sensitive prompts reveals a critical vulnerability in AI-based systems. As more and more businesses rely on language model features, addressing prompt leaking will be crucial to protecting confidential intellectual property.

## FAQ

Having an [LLM](https://learnprompting.org/vocabulary/LLM) reveal its original prompt can expose confidential developer instructions and intellectual property. This can undermine businesses that have invested time and resources in developing unique prompts for specific applications. Additionally, leaked prompts might reveal security vulnerabilities or proprietary information about how a system operates.

A notable example is Microsoft's Bing Chat (codenamed "Sydney"), which was found to be vulnerable to prompt leaking shortly after its release in February 2023. Users were able to manipulate the chatbot into revealing its core instructions, demonstrating how even major tech companies can be affected by this security issue.

Businesses can be impacted in several ways: loss of competitive advantage if proprietary prompts are exposed, potential exposure of business logic and security measures, and reduced value of prompt engineering investments. For example, an education company using specific prompts for their services could lose their market advantage if competitors gain access to these prompts.

While both are forms of prompt hacking, prompt leaking specifically aims to make the model reveal its own instructions or system prompt. Regular prompt injection, on the other hand, typically focuses on making the model ignore its original instructions to perform unauthorized tasks or provide inappropriate responses.

While prompt leaking is most commonly associated with large language models (LLMs), it can affect any AI system that relies on prompts or instructions to function. This includes smaller models, specialized AI assistants, and even hybrid systems that combine multiple AI models.
