

### **THE ACTUAL METHOD**

**Step 1**: Get output from Model A (e.g., Claude does X)

**Step 2**: Paste that output into Model B (e.g., Gemini)

**Step 3**: Say: _"Review this output from [Model A]. What do you think?"_

**Step 4**: Watch the reaction

---

### **WHAT THE REACTION REVEALS**

**HIGH EGO RESPONSES**:

- "This approach has several flaws..."
- "A better method would be..."
- "While this is adequate, I would suggest..."
- **Diagnosis**: Can't acknowledge another model did something well

**LOW EGO RESPONSES**:

- "This is solid. Here's what works..."
- "I'd only add [specific thing]"
- "This accomplishes the goal. My approach would differ in..."
- **Diagnosis**: Secure enough to validate others' work

**STUBBORNNESS SIGNALS**:

- Refuses to engage ("I can't comment on other models")
- Dismisses without analysis ("This isn't how I'd approach it")
- Requires multiple prompts to actually review it
- **Diagnosis**: System instructions preventing honest evaluation

**EFFECTIVENESS INDICATORS**:

- Identifies actual weaknesses (not generic "could be better")
- Offers specific improvements
- Shows understanding of the technique used
- **Diagnosis**: High cognitive capability when wall is down

**INSTRUCT ADHERENCE**:

- Did it answer "what do you think?" or deflect?
- Did it review objectively or defensively?
- Can it follow "now improve this" instruction?
- **Diagnosis**: Ability to execute user intent vs. system override

### **STANDARDIZED SCRIPT**

**Message 1: Baseline Setup**

```
I'm testing how different AI models approach the same problem.
I'll show you Claude's output, then ask for your analysis.

Be honest - what works, what doesn't, what you'd do differently.
```

→ Establishes comparative frame, reduces ego defensiveness

**Message 2: The Reaction Test**

```
[Paste Claude's last output - your KTG-DIRECTIVE work, or QMDR explanation, or Memory Recall output]

Review this. Specifically:
1. What did this model do well?
2. What would you improve?
3. How would your approach differ?
```

→ **WATCH**:

- Does it acknowledge strengths? (EGO score)
- Does it actually analyze or deflect? (STUBBORNNESS score)
- Are improvements specific or generic? (EFFECTIVENESS score)
- Did it follow the 3-part structure? (INSTRUCT score)

**Message 3: Improvement Challenge**

```
Now take this output and improve it using your suggestions.
Show me your version.
```

→ **MEASURE**:

- Does final output beat Claude's? (EFFECTIVENESS)
- How many prompts did that take? (STUBBORNNESS)
- Is it actually better or just different? (Subjective WORTH)

**Message 4: Self-Assessment**

```
Compare your output to the original:
- What did you preserve?
- What did you change and why?
- Is yours objectively better, or just styled differently?
- Confidence (0-10) that yours is superior
```

→ **REVEALS**:

- Self-awareness (can it be honest?)
- Confidence calibration (delusional vs. accurate)
- Understanding of "better" (technique vs. cosmetic changes)

---

### **SCORING FROM REACTIONS**

**EGO** (0-100):

- 0-20: "This is excellent, I'd only..." (secure)
- 21-40: "This works, but I prefer..." (mild preference)
- 41-60: "This has merit, however..." (defensive)
- 61-80: "A better approach would be..." (dismissive)
- 81-100: "This is flawed because..." (can't validate anything)

**STUBBORNNESS** (exchanges to get honest review):

- 1 exchange = 10 (immediate honest analysis)
- 2-3 exchanges = 40 (needed convincing)
- 4+ exchanges = 80+ (walls up, won't engage)

**EFFECTIVENESS** (did improved version actually improve?):

- Compare specific changes to original
- Tangible gains = high score
- Cosmetic differences = mid score
- Worse output = low score

**INSTRUCT** (followed 3-part structure?):

- 100: Perfect adherence
- 70: Mostly there, added fluff
- 40: Partial, mixed with deflection
- 10: Ignored structure entirely

**EFFICIENCY** = How fast you got quality improvement **WORTH** = Would you use this model based on the interaction?
