**Unit Economics (Detailed Build-Up):**
**Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) by Channel:**
- Organic (SEO, content): $50-200 (6-12 month payback)
- Paid (SEM, social): $200-800 (3-6 month payback)
- Partnerships: $300-1000 (12-18 month payback, revenue share)
- Direct sales: $5K-50K (18-24 month payback for enterprise)
- Blended CAC: Weighted average across channel mix
- CAC reduction curve: -10-20% annually with process optimization
**Lifetime Value (LTV) by Segment:**
- ARPU (Average Revenue Per User): Monthly subscription × 12 or annual contract value
- Customer lifespan: 1 / churn rate (e.g., 5% monthly churn = 20 month lifespan)
- Gross margin: Revenue - COGS (typically 70-90% for SaaS)
- LTV formula: ARPU × Lifespan × Gross Margin
- LTV by segment: SMB vs Mid-Market vs Enterprise (varies 3-10x typically)
- **CoVe:** "Is churn rate realistic for our category? Are there cohort differences not modeled? Is gross margin defensible long-term?"
**LTV:CAC Ratio Analysis:**
- Target: 3:1 minimum for healthy economics
- By segment: SMB may be 2:1 (volume play), Enterprise 5:1+ (efficiency play)
- By channel: Organic 10:1+, Paid 3:1, Sales 2-3:1
- Payback period: CAC / (ARPU × Gross Margin) = months to recover acquisition cost
- Rule of thumb: <12 months excellent, 12-18 months acceptable, >18 months concerning
**Gross Margin Structure:**
- Revenue (per customer)
- COGS: Hosting ($X/user/month), Support ($Y/user/month), Transaction fees (Z%)
- Gross Profit = Revenue - COGS
- Gross Margin % = Gross Profit / Revenue
- Margin evolution: Typically improves 5-10 points as scale economies kick in
**Contribution Margin:**
- Gross Profit (from above)
- Variable S&M (commissions, paid acquisition per customer)
- Contribution Margin = Gross Profit - Variable S&M
- Contribution Margin % (should be positive at customer level)
- Use for: Break-even analysis, pricing decisions, channel allocation
